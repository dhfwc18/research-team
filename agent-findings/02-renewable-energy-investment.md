# Topic 2: Renewable Energy Investment Trends

## Overview

Sources covering global renewable energy investment flows, capacity statistics, and financing
analysis from IRENA, IEA, BloombergNEF, REN21, World Bank, and academic literature.

---

## Government and Institutional Sources

### 1. IRENA — Global Landscape of Energy Transition Finance 2025
- **URL:** https://www.irena.org/Publications/2025/Nov/Global-landscape-of-energy-transition-finance-2025
- **Organization:** International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA) and Climate Policy Initiative (CPI)
- **Date:** November 2025
- **Relevance:** Fourth edition flagship joint finance report; tracks all energy transition
  investment flows globally, including renewable energy ($807 billion in 2024), grids, storage,
  and efficiency. Highlights regional disparities between advanced and developing economies.

### 2. IRENA — Renewable Capacity Statistics 2025
- **URL:** https://www.irena.org/Publications/2025/Mar/Renewable-capacity-statistics-2025
- **Organization:** International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA)
- **Date:** March 2025
- **Relevance:** Annual dataset on installed renewable power capacity by country and technology;
  records global capacity reaching 4,448 GW at end-2024 with 585 GW of net additions. Primary
  reference for global capacity addition data.

### 3. IRENA — Renewable Energy Statistics 2025
- **URL:** https://www.irena.org/Publications/2025/Jul/Renewable-energy-statistics-2025
- **Organization:** International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA)
- **Date:** July 2025
- **Relevance:** Comprehensive statistical compendium covering capacity, generation, and
  investment across all renewable technologies and regions; includes 2025 data showing global
  capacity at 5,149 GW after a record 692 GW added in 2025.

### 4. IRENA — Global Renewable Energy Investment Hit USD 807 Billion in 2024 (Press Release)
- **URL:** https://www.irena.org/News/pressreleases/2025/Nov/Global-Renewable-Energy-Investment-Hit-USD-807-Billion-in-2024
- **Organization:** International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA)
- **Date:** November 2025
- **Relevance:** Official IRENA press release with 2024 investment summary. Solar accounted for
  $554 billion (69% of total); year-on-year growth slowed to 7.3% from 32% the prior year.
  Key headline statistics source.

### 5. IEA — World Energy Investment 2024
- **URL:** https://www.iea.org/reports/world-energy-investment-2024
- **Organization:** International Energy Agency (IEA)
- **Date:** June 2024
- **Relevance:** Annual benchmark report; finds global energy investment surpassing $3 trillion
  for first time, with clean energy spending at a 2:1 ratio to fossil fuels. Includes regional
  breakdowns for China, EU, US, and emerging markets.

### 6. IEA — World Energy Investment 2025
- **URL:** https://www.iea.org/reports/world-energy-investment-2025
- **Organization:** International Energy Agency (IEA)
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Projects global energy investment rising to $3.3 trillion in 2025, with $2.2
  trillion directed to clean energy; highlights grid investment gaps and China's growing share
  of global clean energy spending (nearly one-third).

### 7. IEA — Renewables 2024: Analysis and Forecast to 2030
- **URL:** https://www.iea.org/reports/renewables-2024
- **Organization:** International Energy Agency (IEA)
- **Date:** October 2024
- **Relevance:** Annual market forecast covering renewable capacity additions through 2030;
  projects global additions rising from 666 GW in 2024 to 935 GW by 2030, with solar accounting
  for 80% of new capacity. Country-level forecasts for China, EU, India, and the US.

### 8. World Bank — Regulatory Indicators for Sustainable Energy (RISE) 2024
- **URL:** https://documents.worldbank.org/en/publication/documents-reports/documentdetail/099051625184514754
- **Organization:** World Bank / ESMAP
- **Date:** 2025 (covering 2024 data)
- **Relevance:** Policy and regulatory benchmark assessing 140 countries across renewable energy,
  energy efficiency, and energy access; 28 indicators and 158 data points per country. Useful
  for understanding investment climate drivers and barriers across emerging markets.

## Industry Reports

### 9. BloombergNEF — Energy Transition Investment Trends 2025
- **URL:** https://assets.bbhub.io/professional/sites/24/951623_BNEF-Energy-Transition-Trends-2025-Abridged.pdf
- **Organization:** BloombergNEF
- **Date:** January 2025
- **Relevance:** Tracks global low-carbon investment reaching a record $2.1 trillion in 2024,
  broken down by sector (electrified transport, renewables, grids, storage). Covers 130 countries
  and 17 categories of clean energy technologies.

### 10. BloombergNEF — Global Energy Transition Investment Reached Record $2.3 Trillion in 2025
- **URL:** https://about.bnef.com/insights/clean-energy/bloombergnef-finds-global-energy-transition-investment-reached-record-2-3-trillion-in-2025-up-8-from-2024/
- **Organization:** BloombergNEF
- **Date:** 2026
- **Relevance:** Reports 2025 full-year energy transition investment up 8% to $2.3 trillion;
  electrified transport leads at $893 billion. Notes China's first decline in renewables funding
  since 2013 alongside EU growth of 18%. Most current trend data available.

### 11. REN21 — Renewables 2024 Global Status Report: Investment and Finance Module
- **URL:** https://www.ren21.net/gsr-2024/modules/global_overview/03_investment/
- **Organization:** REN21 (Renewable Energy Policy Network for the 21st Century)
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Multi-stakeholder annual report covering global renewable investment reaching
  $623 billion in 2023 (up 8.1%); includes analysis of financing gaps, cost-of-capital barriers
  in developing economies, and grid infrastructure bottlenecks.

## Academic Papers

### 12. Determinants of International Development Investments in Renewable Energy in Developing
Countries
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0973082623000790
- **Date:** 2023 (Energy for Sustainable Development)
- **Relevance:** Panel-data study identifying causal factors -- governance quality, GDP, trade
  openness, technological innovation, and fossil fuel dependence -- behind renewable energy
  investment flows into developing nations.

### 13. Navigating the Energy Crisis: Unveiling the Determinants of Renewable Energy Investment
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0360544225028816
- **Date:** 2025 (Energy)
- **Relevance:** Econometric study across 23 countries (2000-2020) examining how AI adoption,
  energy uncertainty, energy depletion, and financial development drive renewable investment;
  finds AI consistently enhances renewable investment across development stages.

### 14. Reducing the Cost of Capital Through International Climate Finance
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0301421524001241
- **Date:** 2024 (Energy Policy)
- **Relevance:** Analyzes how concessional international climate finance instruments can reduce
  cost-of-capital barriers in emerging markets, a key structural constraint on renewable
  deployment in the Global South.
