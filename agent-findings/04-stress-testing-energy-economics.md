# Topic 4: Stress Testing Methodologies for Energy Economics

## Overview

Sources covering stress testing frameworks, climate scenario analysis, and sensitivity
analysis methodologies applied to the energy sector, energy investments, and the financial
system's exposure to energy transition risks. Key institutions: NGFS, ECB, IMF, World Bank,
BIS, Deutsche Bundesbank, De Nederlandsche Bank, IEA.

---

## NGFS Climate Scenarios (Primary Benchmark Framework)

### 1. NGFS — Climate Scenarios for Central Banks and Supervisors, Phase V
- **URL:** https://www.ngfs.net/en/publications-and-statistics/publications/ngfs-climate-scenarios-central-banks-and-supervisors-phase-v
- **Organization:** Network for Greening the Financial System (NGFS)
- **Date:** November 2024
- **Relevance:** Fifth and most current vintage of the canonical NGFS long-term climate scenario
  suite (Net Zero 2050, Delayed Transition, Current Policies, etc.), used as primary benchmark
  for climate stress testing by central banks and financial supervisors globally. Includes
  updated physical risk modeling and country-level NDC commitments as of March 2024.

### 2. NGFS — Climate Scenarios Technical Documentation Phase IV (V4.2)
- **URL:** https://www.ngfs.net/sites/default/files/media/2024/01/16/ngfs_scenarios_technical_documentation_phase_iv_2023.pdf
- **Organization:** NGFS / PIK / IIASA / University of Maryland / NIESR
- **Date:** November 2023
- **Relevance:** Full technical documentation of NGFS scenario methodology for Phase IV,
  covering model structure, scenario design logic, energy transition pathways, and macroeconomic
  transmission. Essential reference for understanding how NGFS scenarios are constructed as
  stress test inputs.

## Central Bank and Institutional Stress Test Frameworks

### 3. ECB — The Road to Paris: Stress Testing the Transition Towards a Net-Zero Economy
(Occasional Paper No. 328)
- **URL:** https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpops/ecb.op328~2c44ee718e.en.pdf
- **Authors:** Emambakhsh, Fuchs, Kordel, Kouratzoglou, Lelli, Pizzeghello, Salleo, Spaggiari
- **Organization:** European Central Bank
- **Date:** September 2023
- **Relevance:** Second ECB economy-wide climate stress test. Introduces three short-term
  transition scenarios combining NGFS pathways with macroeconomic projections and granular
  energy-sector dynamics by country. Covers impacts on euro area firms, households, and banks.
  One of the most methodologically complete operational stress testing frameworks available.

### 4. ECB — Economy-Wide Climate Stress Test (Occasional Paper No. 281)
- **URL:** https://www.ecb.europa.eu/pub/pdf/scpops/ecb.op281~05a7735b1c.en.pdf
- **Organization:** European Central Bank
- **Date:** September 2021
- **Relevance:** Foundational ECB climate stress test covering over four million firms and 1,600
  euro area banks across three NGFS-derived scenarios. Establishes top-down methodology for
  translating climate transition and physical risk scenarios into firm creditworthiness and bank
  loss estimates.

### 5. BIS FSI — Stress-Testing Banks for Climate Change: A Comparison of Practices
(FSI Insights No. 34)
- **URL:** https://www.bis.org/fsi/publ/insights34.pdf
- **Authors:** Patrizia Baudino and Jean-Philippe Svoronos
- **Organization:** Bank for International Settlements / Financial Stability Institute
- **Date:** July 2021
- **Relevance:** Comparative review of climate stress testing methodologies across jurisdictions,
  identifying key challenges (data gaps, long time horizons, transition uncertainty) and how
  they have been addressed in practice. Directly relevant to benchmarking stress testing
  framework design.

### 6. DNB — An Energy Transition Risk Stress Test for the Financial System of the Netherlands
- **URL:** https://www.dnb.nl/media/pdnpdalc/201810_nr-_7_-2018-_an_energy_transition_risk_stress_test_for_the_financial_system_of_the_netherlands.pdf
- **Authors:** Vermeulen, Schets et al.
- **Organization:** De Nederlandsche Bank
- **Date:** October 2018
- **Relevance:** Pioneering central-bank energy transition stress test. Analyzes four scenarios
  (policy shock, technology shock, combined shock, confidence shock) across Dutch banks,
  insurers, and pension funds. Widely cited as methodological template for subsequent
  energy-sector transition stress tests.

### 7. Deutsche Bundesbank — Climate Transition Risk Stress Test for the German Financial System
- **URL:** https://www.bundesbank.de/en/publications/reports/technical-papers/climate-transition-risk-stress-test-for-the-german-financial-system-922198
- **Organization:** Deutsche Bundesbank
- **Date:** April 2023
- **Relevance:** Models orderly (net-zero by 2050) and disorderly (abrupt carbon price shock to
  EUR 200/tonne) transition scenarios and their impact on non-financial corporate default
  probabilities and German bank loan portfolios. Detailed treatment of fossil energy sector
  transmission channels.

## IMF Stress Testing and Scenario Analysis

### 8. IMF — Climate-Related Stress Testing: Transition Risks in Norway (WP 20/232)
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2020/11/08/Climate-Related-Stress-Testing-Transition-Risks-in-Norway-49835
- **Authors:** Grippa and Mann
- **Organization:** International Monetary Fund
- **Date:** November 2020
- **Relevance:** IMF stress testing framework applied to Norway with three transmission channels:
  domestic carbon price impacts on firms, global carbon price impacts on the oil sector, and
  forced output reductions on shareholder portfolios. Key methodological reference for
  energy-exporting economies.

### 9. IMF — Climate-Related Stress Testing: Transition Risk in Colombia (WP 21/261)
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2021/11/05/Climate-Related-Stress-Testing-Transition-Risk-in-Colombia-504344
- **Organization:** International Monetary Fund
- **Date:** November 2021
- **Relevance:** First IMF climate stress test applied to an emerging market economy. Uses firm-
  and bank-level data to quantify financial stability implications of carbon tax scenarios
  through banks' corporate loan exposures. Establishes template for FSAP-integrated climate
  stress testing.

### 10. IMF — Not All Energy Transitions Are Alike (WP 23/160)
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WP/Issues/2023/08/04/Not-All-Energy-Transitions-Are-Alike-Disentangling-the-Effects-of-Demand-and-Supply-Side-536913
- **Authors:** Lukas Boer, Andrea Pescatori, Martin Stuermer
- **Organization:** International Monetary Fund
- **Date:** August 2023
- **Relevance:** Structural scenario analysis showing demand-side vs. supply-side climate
  policies produce radically different oil price trajectories ($25 vs. $130/barrel in 2030
  under net-zero). Directly applicable as a stress test scenario design tool for energy
  investment analysis.

### 11. IMF — Energy Transition and Geoeconomic Fragmentation (Staff Climate Note 2023/003)
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/staff-climate-notes/Issues/2023/11/16/Energy-Transition-and-Geoeconomic-Fragmentation-Implications-for-Climate-Scenario-Design-541097
- **Authors:** Gardes-Landolfini, Grippa, Oman, Yu
- **Organization:** International Monetary Fund
- **Date:** November 2023
- **Relevance:** Examines how post-COVID supply chain disruptions, Ukraine energy crisis, and
  geoeconomic fragmentation alter plausibility of existing NGFS/IEA transition scenarios.
  Argues for stress scenario updates to reflect carbon lock-in risks and commodity market
  fragmentation. Relevant to scenario validity in energy stress testing.

### 12. IMF — Assessing Stress from Oil Price Shocks (COVID-19 Special Series)
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/publications/covid19-special-notes/en-special-series-on-covid-19-assessing-stress-from-oil-price-shocks.pdf
- **Organization:** International Monetary Fund (Monetary and Capital Markets Department)
- **Date:** April 2020
- **Relevance:** Operational note on translating oil price shocks into macroeconomic stress
  scenarios for bank stress tests. Covers scenario articulation process, adjustment paths, and
  key variable selection (unemployment, interest rates, exchange rates). Useful methodological
  reference for energy price shock stress test design.

## World Bank and Sensitivity Analysis Methodology

### 13. World Bank — Climate Transition Risk Stress Testing Framework
- **URL:** https://documents1.worldbank.org/curated/en/099110525111028579/pdf/P504359-a7b462b8-4814-4573-aaed-545cc2b89af9.pdf
- **Organization:** World Bank
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** Operational World Bank framework for quantifying climate transition risk in
  banking sectors; application to Albanian banking sector. Focuses on carbon tax as policy
  instrument and models transmission through the energy sector to financial institution balance
  sheets.

### 14. IEA — Global Energy and Climate Model: Scenario Documentation (STEPS / APS / NZE)
- **URL:** https://www.iea.org/reports/global-energy-and-climate-model
- **Organization:** International Energy Agency (IEA)
- **Date:** Continuously updated (current as of WEO 2024/2025)
- **Relevance:** Authoritative documentation of IEA three core scenarios -- Stated Policies
  (STEPS), Announced Pledges (APS), and Net Zero Emissions by 2050 (NZE) -- which serve as
  principal benchmark energy transition pathways for stress testing energy investments.
  Includes sector-by-sector and region-by-region projections.

### 15. Usher et al. — Global Sensitivity Analysis for Energy System Optimisation Modelling
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10445882/
- **Authors:** Usher et al.
- **Organization:** Open Research Europe / EU Horizon 2020 ECEMF programme
- **Date:** February 2023 (published; PMC indexed August 2023)
- **Relevance:** Provides methodological guidelines for global sensitivity analysis (Morris
  method) applied to energy system optimization models (ESOMs/OSeMOSYS). Addresses how
  uncertainty propagation and key driver identification should be conducted in long-term energy
  scenario models -- applicable as a stress test sensitivity methodology.
