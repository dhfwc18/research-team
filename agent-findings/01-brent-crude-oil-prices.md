# Topic 1: Brent Crude Oil Price Trends and Forecasts

## Overview

Sources covering historical Brent crude price data, forecast methodologies, and institutional
outlooks from government agencies, multilateral institutions, and academic literature.

---

## Government and Institutional Sources

### 1. EIA — Europe Brent Spot Price FOB (Monthly Historical Data)
- **URL:** https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/hist/rbrtem.htm
- **Organization:** U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)
- **Date:** Updated monthly (data from May 1987 to present; last release April 1, 2026)
- **Relevance:** Primary authoritative dataset of monthly Brent crude spot prices covering
  nearly four decades. Downloadable as XLS. Also links to daily and weekly series.

### 2. EIA — Spot Prices for Crude Oil and Petroleum Products
- **URL:** https://www.eia.gov/dnav/pet/pet_pri_spt_s1_d.htm
- **Organization:** U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)
- **Date:** Continuously updated
- **Relevance:** Comprehensive EIA hub for daily spot prices of Brent crude, WTI, and
  petroleum products. Canonical source for US and international crude price series at daily
  granularity.

### 3. EIA — Short-Term Energy Outlook (STEO), March 2026
- **URL:** https://www.eia.gov/outlooks/steo/pdf/steo_full.pdf
- **Organization:** U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)
- **Date:** March 2026
- **Relevance:** EIA flagship near-term price forecast projecting Brent and WTI prices 12-18
  months out. Includes supply/demand balances, OPEC+ assumptions, and scenario analysis.
  Published monthly.

### 4. IEA — Oil 2025: Analysis and Forecast to 2030
- **URL:** https://iea.blob.core.windows.net/assets/c0087308-f434-4284-b5bb-bfaf745c81c3/Oil2025.pdf
- **Organization:** International Energy Agency (IEA)
- **Date:** 2025
- **Relevance:** IEA medium-term oil market forecast covering supply, demand, refining, and
  trade through 2030. Provides IEA official structured price outlook under multiple policy
  scenarios.

### 5. IEA — World Energy Outlook 2024
- **URL:** https://www.iea.org/reports/world-energy-outlook-2024
- **Organization:** International Energy Agency (IEA)
- **Date:** October 2024
- **Relevance:** Long-term energy and oil market scenarios to 2050. Identifies structural
  overhang of oil supply emerging in the late 2020s and projects spare crude capacity rising to
  8 mb/d by 2030, with implications for long-run price levels.

### 6. World Bank — Commodity Markets Outlook, October 2025
- **URL:** https://openknowledge.worldbank.org/entities/publication/d08d3ee8-e38a-4f02-8ade-95b9c6075c0b
- **Organization:** World Bank
- **Date:** October 2025
- **Relevance:** Forecasts Brent at $68/bbl in 2025 and $60/bbl in 2026 -- a six-year low --
  driven by a global oil glut of 2.7 mb/d. Includes detailed supply/demand decompositions and
  risk scenarios.

### 7. World Bank — Commodity Markets Outlook, April 2025
- **URL:** https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/1b388949805c9a0ae3736bdacb32ea94-0050012025/original/CMO-April-2025.pdf
- **Organization:** World Bank
- **Date:** April 2025
- **Relevance:** Mid-year World Bank commodity price assessment with Brent projections and
  discussion of OPEC+ dynamics, geopolitical risks, and demand-side headwinds. Useful for
  tracking revision trajectory.

### 8. IMF — World Economic Outlook, October 2025
- **URL:** https://www.imf.org/-/media/files/publications/weo/2025/october/english/text.pdf
- **Organization:** International Monetary Fund (IMF)
- **Date:** October 2025
- **Relevance:** Contains IMF futures-based oil price assumptions: $68.92/bbl in 2025 and
  $65.84/bbl in 2026 (down from $79.17/bbl in 2024). Used as baseline price inputs for global
  growth and fiscal projections.

### 9. EIA — Brent Crude Oil Prices Averaged $19 Per Barrel Less in 2023 Than 2022
- **URL:** https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=61142
- **Organization:** U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA)
- **Date:** January 2024
- **Relevance:** Annual retrospective analysis of 2023 Brent price trends, covering geopolitical
  and supply/demand drivers of the year-on-year price decline from $100/bbl to $82/bbl. Useful
  for recent historical context.

## Datasets

### 10. Our World in Data — Crude Oil Spot Prices (Inflation-Adjusted)
- **URL:** https://ourworldindata.org/grapher/crude-oil-spot-prices
- **Organization:** Our World in Data (sourced from Energy Institute / S&P Global Platts
  Statistical Review of World Energy 2025)
- **Date:** Updated 2025 (data from 1861)
- **Relevance:** Longest available historical crude oil price series (165+ years), both nominal
  and inflation-adjusted. Downloadable as CSV. Ideal for multi-decade trend analyses and
  visualization.

## Academic Papers

### 11. Hamilton, J.D. — "Understanding Crude Oil Prices" (NBER Working Paper 14492)
- **URL:** https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w14492/w14492.pdf
- **Author:** James D. Hamilton
- **Date:** November 2008
- **Relevance:** Foundational academic paper examining statistical behavior of oil prices over
  the long run, covering OPEC, resource depletion, commodity speculation, and demand elasticity.
  Essential reference for oil price determinants literature.

### 12. Kilian, L. — "Not All Oil Price Shocks Are Alike" (AER 2009)
- **URL:** https://www.aeaweb.org/articles?id=10.1257/aer.99.3.1053
- **Author:** Lutz Kilian
- **Date:** 2009 (American Economic Review, Vol. 99, No. 3, pp. 1053-1069)
- **Relevance:** Seminal structural VAR paper decomposing oil price shocks into supply shocks,
  global demand shocks, and oil-specific demand shocks. Dominant methodological framework for
  causal analysis of oil price movements.

### 13. arXiv 2409.12376 — Prediction of Brent Crude Oil Price Based on LSTM Model
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.12376
- **Authors:** Zhang et al.
- **Date:** September 2024
- **Relevance:** Targets Brent crude price forecasting using a three-layer LSTM deep learning
  model trained on EIA spot price data. Tests performance across periods of sharp price
  fluctuation in the energy transition context.

### 14. arXiv 2306.03052 — Forecasting Crude Oil Prices Using Reservoir Computing Models
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2306.03052
- **Author:** Kaushal Kumar
- **Date:** June 2023
- **Relevance:** Benchmarks reservoir computing against LSTM for crude oil price forecasting on
  2010-2023 data. Finds reservoir computing outperforms LSTM on prediction error and speed.
  Methodological comparison paper.

### 15. arXiv 2405.19849 — Modelling and Forecasting Energy Market Volatility Using GARCH and ML
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2405.19849v1
- **Date:** 2024
- **Relevance:** Comparative study of GARCH-family models vs. machine learning for crude oil,
  gasoline, heating oil, and natural gas volatility forecasting. Finds ML superior out-of-sample.
  Directly relevant to oil price volatility methodology.
