# Topic 3: Microeconomic Models Linking Oil Prices to Renewable Energy Substitution

## Overview

Sources covering the academic literature on econometric and microeconomic models of the
relationship between fossil fuel prices and renewable energy investment or substitution.
Includes interfuel substitution elasticity papers, structural VAR decompositions, and
panel data studies across countries and firms.

---

## Foundational Models and Elasticity Papers

### 1. Papageorgiou, Saam, Schulte — Substitution Between Clean and Dirty Energy Inputs
- **URL:** https://direct.mit.edu/rest/article-abstract/99/2/281/58376/Substitution-between-Clean-and-Dirty-Energy-Inputs
- **Authors:** Chris Papageorgiou, Marianne Saam, Patrick Schulte
- **Date:** 2017 (Review of Economics and Statistics, Vol. 99, No. 2, pp. 281-290)
- **Relevance:** Landmark panel study across 26 countries estimating macroeconomic elasticity of
  substitution between clean and dirty energy inputs using nested CES production functions.
  Finds elasticity significantly exceeds one (~1.8 in electricity sectors). Critical parameter
  for renewable substitution modeling.

### 2. Hossain, Serletis — A Century of Interfuel Substitution
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3048661
- **Authors:** A K M Nurul Hossain, Apostolos Serletis
- **Date:** 2017 (Journal of Commodity Markets, Vol. 8, pp. 28-42)
- **Relevance:** Estimates interfuel substitution elasticities across US fossil fuel types
  (1919-2012) using Normalized Quadratic expenditure function with time-varying error
  covariance. Establishes baseline substitution parameters for modeling how price shocks in
  one fuel affect demand for others including renewables.

### 3. Ma, Stern — Long-Run Estimates of Interfuel and Interfactor Elasticities
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2724312
- **Authors:** Chunbo Ma, David I. Stern
- **Date:** 2016 (Resource and Energy Economics, Vol. 46, pp. 114-130)
- **Relevance:** Uses between-estimator and long-run difference estimators on Chinese provincial
  panel data to produce long-run interfuel substitution elasticities. Shows estimates are
  sensitive to data type (time series vs. panel vs. cross-section). Directly relevant to
  methodology choice in energy transition econometrics.

### 4. Chakravorty, Tse — Transition from Fossil Fuels to Renewable Energy (SSRN / FEEM)
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=200149
- **Authors:** Ujjayant Chakravorty, Kinping Tse
- **Date:** 1999 (SSRN; FEEM publication)
- **Relevance:** Foundational partial equilibrium model of the energy sector with exhaustible
  resources (oil, coal, gas) and endogenous renewable substitution. Finds coal most sensitive
  to solar cost reductions while oil and gas are relatively inelastic.

### 5. Stockl — Is Substitutability the New Efficiency? (DIW Berlin Discussion Paper 1886)
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3671370
- **Author:** Fabian Stockl
- **Date:** 2020 (DIW Berlin Discussion Paper No. 1886)
- **Relevance:** Microeconomic growth model where the elasticity of substitution between clean
  and dirty energy is endogenous through investment. Demonstrates that with sufficient
  substitutability investment, fossil fuels become inessential and income shares decline,
  generating an endogenous transition path.

### 6. Mercure, Salas — Global Economic Potentials and Marginal Costs of Non-Renewable Resources
- **URL:** https://arxiv.org/abs/1209.0708
- **Authors:** Jean-Francois Mercure, Pablo Salas
- **Date:** 2012 (arXiv:1209.0708; published 2013)
- **Relevance:** Develops simulation model for evolution of marginal production costs of fossil
  fuel energy carriers and long-term average price formation integrated into the FTT:Power
  global energy model. Provides theoretical price-cost framework underlying fossil-to-renewable
  transition economics.

## Econometric Studies: Oil Prices and Renewable Investment

### 7. Zaghdoudi et al. — Asymmetric Responses to Oil Price Uncertainty in US Low-Carbon Energy R&D
- **URL:** https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6345355
- **Author:** Taha Zaghdoudi
- **Date:** 2024 (SSRN working paper)
- **Relevance:** Uses ARDL bounds testing and Fourier-augmented NARDL on US data (1974-2022)
  to show oil price uncertainty has symmetric negative long-run effects on renewable R&D
  investment, regardless of whether shocks are positive or negative.

### 8. Zaghdoudi et al. — Asymmetric Connectedness Between Oil Price, Coal and Renewable Energy in China
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0360544223028104
- **Authors:** Taha Zaghdoudi, Kais Tissaoui, Mohamed Hedi Maaloul, Younes Bahou, Niazi Kammoun
- **Date:** 2023 (Energy)
- **Relevance:** Fourier nonlinear ARDL model showing oil price increases stimulate long-run
  renewable energy consumption in China while simultaneously boosting short-run coal demand.
  Provides direct econometric estimate of asymmetric substitution dynamics.

### 9. Mukhtarov et al. — Oil Prices and the Renewable Energy Transition: Evidence from China
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0957178724001346
- **Authors:** Shahriyar Mukhtarov et al.
- **Date:** 2024 (Utilities Policy, Vol. 91)
- **Relevance:** Canonical cointegrating regression on China data (1990-2020) quantifies that a
  1% increase in oil prices raises renewable energy consumption by 0.16%. Directly estimates
  price-driven substitution between fossil fuels and renewables.

### 10. Esmaeili et al. — From Oil Surges to Renewable Shifts (Energy Policy 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0301421524002726
- **Authors:** Parisa Esmaeili, Meysam Rafei, Mahmoud Salari, Daniel Balsalobre-Lorente
- **Date:** 2024 (Energy Policy)
- **Relevance:** Decomposes crude oil market shocks (supply, aggregate demand, oil-specific
  demand) over 1973-2021; finds oil-specific demand shocks ultimately accelerate clean energy
  adoption through elevated price transmission.

### 11. Applied Economics (2025) — Non-linear Relationship Between Oil Price Uncertainty and
Renewable Energy Transitions
- **URL:** https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/00036846.2025.2500710
- **Date:** 2025 (Applied Economics, Vol. 0, online first)
- **Relevance:** Panel study of 71 countries (1980-2022) documenting N-shaped nonlinear
  relationship between oil price uncertainty and renewable energy transition. Human capital and
  green innovation act as moderating variables; significant only in OECD economies.

### 12. Cao, Sun, Guo — Asymmetric Effect of Oil Price Uncertainty on Corporate Investment
- **URL:** https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/energy-research/articles/10.3389/fenrg.2020.00047/full
- **Authors:** Hong Cao, Pengfei Sun, Litian Guo
- **Date:** 2020 (Frontiers in Energy Research)
- **Relevance:** Fixed-effects microeconomic study of listed Chinese renewable energy firms
  (2000-2017); shows oil price uncertainty significantly reduces corporate investment, with
  asymmetric responses contingent on firm-level investment opportunity sets.

### 13. Ozdurak — Nexus Between Crude Oil Prices, Clean Energy Investments and Technology (Green Finance 2021)
- **URL:** https://www.aimspress.com/article/doi/10.3934/GF.2021017?viewType=HTML
- **Author:** Caner Ozdurak
- **Date:** 2021 (Green Finance, Vol. 3, No. 3, pp. 337-350)
- **Relevance:** DCC-GARCH modeling on daily data (2009-2021) shows oil price declines reduce
  clean energy stock performance, while technology sector prices positively co-move with
  renewable energy investment indices.

### 14. MDPI Sustainability (2025) — Oil Prices, Financial Development, and Urbanization in E-10
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/17/22/10242
- **Date:** 2025 (Sustainability, Vol. 17, No. 22)
- **Relevance:** Dynamic GMM panel analysis of 10 emerging economies finding that oil prices
  exert adverse pressure on renewable energy adoption while financial development and economic
  growth are key facilitators; provides demand-elasticity framing for the energy transition.

### 15. Magazzino, Giolli — Oil Prices and Renewable Energy Sources in Italy (Renewable Energy Focus 2024)
- **URL:** https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1755008424000085
- **Authors:** Cosimo Magazzino, Lorenzo Giolli
- **Date:** 2024 (Renewable Energy Focus, Vol. 48)
- **Relevance:** Uses spectral causality and wavelet coherence on Italian time-series data to
  demonstrate that solar, hydro, and wind energy outputs Granger-cause oil prices. Provides
  causal time-frequency evidence of the renewable-to-fossil feedback mechanism.
