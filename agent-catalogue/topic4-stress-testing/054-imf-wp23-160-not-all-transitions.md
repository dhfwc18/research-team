# IMF WP/23/160 -- Not All Energy Transitions Are Alike

**Authors:** Lukas Boer, Andrea Pescatori, Martin Stuermer
**Organisation:** International Monetary Fund
**Date:** August 2023

## Description

IMF Working Paper disentangling the effects of demand-side vs supply-side energy transitions
on macroeconomic outcomes. Page returned 403. Directly parallels Kilian (2009) for the
energy transition context. Distinguishes between: (1) demand-side transitions (policy-driven
clean energy adoption reducing fossil demand) and (2) supply-side transitions (technology-
driven cost reductions in renewables). Key finding likely that demand-side transitions (policy-
driven) have different macro consequences than supply-side (technology-driven).

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- The supply vs demand decomposition in the energy transition context directly
maps onto the project's objective. Understanding whether Brent price changes are driven by
demand or supply determines the direction of renewable investment effects.

## Possible Application

- Demand vs supply transition decomposition for scenario design
- Macro consequences of different transition pathways for stress-testing
- Bridge between Kilian (2009) shock decomposition and the energy transition literature
