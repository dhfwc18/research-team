# NGFS -- Climate Scenarios for Central Banks and Supervisors, Phase V

**Organisation:** Network for Greening the Financial System (NGFS)
**Date:** November 2024 (updated February 2026)

## Description

The NGFS Phase V scenarios are the leading international framework for climate-related
financial risk assessment. Provides forward-looking pathways exploring global economy and
financial system evolution under different climate policy ambition levels and physical
climate impacts. Full dataset accessible via NGFS IIASA Scenario Explorer with NACE, GIGS,
and IEA variable mappings. Technical documentation: 8.82 MB. Note: Phase V's physical damage
estimates (Kotz et al. 2024) were retracted from Nature; updated methodology expected
end-2026.

## Key Parameters Available (from documentation)

- Multiple scenarios varying policy ambition (orderly/disorderly/hot house)
- Macroeconomic variables, carbon prices, oil/energy price paths, renewable investment trajectories
- Sector-level transition risk assessments

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- The NGFS scenarios are the standard reference framework for climate-related
stress testing. Directly applicable as the macroeconomic scenario backbone for the Brent-
renewable analysis stress tests.

## Possible Application

- Macroeconomic scenario framework (orderly/disorderly/hot house) for stress-testing
- Oil price paths by scenario from the NGFS IIASA Explorer
- Carbon price trajectories as Brent price interaction variable
