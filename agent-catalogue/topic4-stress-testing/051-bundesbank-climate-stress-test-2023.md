# Deutsche Bundesbank -- Climate Transition Risk Stress Test for the German Financial System

**Authors:** Ivan Frankovic, Tobias Etzel, Alexander Falter, Christian Gross, Jana Ohls,
Lena Strobel, Hannes Wilke
**Organisation:** Deutsche Bundesbank
**Date:** Technical Paper 04/2023 (published January 19, 2024)

## Description

Comprehensive climate transition risk stress test for the German financial sector. Examines
how climate transition risks (policy changes, technology shifts, market adjustments) affect
credit portfolios, asset valuations, and financial stability. Aligned with the Bundesbank's
commitment to supporting the low-carbon transition. PDF download available (1 MB).
Employs multi-sector, multi-scenario approach consistent with NGFS scenario framework.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Provides a recent (2024) methodological example of a comprehensive transition
risk stress test. The German financial system's heavy industrial lending exposure makes this
particularly relevant for understanding oil-sector stranded asset dynamics.

## Possible Application

- Transition risk stress-test methodology for the project's scenario design
- Credit portfolio impact assessment framework
- Germany/Europe as reference jurisdiction for the report
