# DNB -- An Energy Transition Risk Stress Test for the Financial System of the Netherlands

**Organisation:** De Nederlandsche Bank
**Date:** October 2018

## Description

Pioneering energy-transition-specific stress test for the Dutch financial system. The PDF
returned 403 on fetch. The DNB 2018 study is a landmark in energy transition stress testing:
it was one of the first to specifically model the financial risks of the energy transition
(as distinct from physical climate risks), using three scenarios with different carbon price
trajectories and fossil fuel demand assumptions. Results quantified stranded asset losses
and bank credit risk exposure.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Foundational reference for energy transition risk stress testing. The three-
scenario approach (no transition, delayed transition, rapid transition) maps directly onto
the project's stress-test scenario design.

## Possible Application

- Three-scenario structure (no/delayed/rapid transition) for stress-test design
- Stranded asset quantification methodology
- Carbon price and fossil fuel demand interaction modelling
