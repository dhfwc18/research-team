# IMF -- Energy Transition and Geoeconomic Fragmentation (Staff Climate Note 2023/003)

**Organisation:** International Monetary Fund
**Date:** November 2023

## Description

IMF Staff Climate Note examining how geoeconomic fragmentation (trade and investment
balkanisation, geopolitical tensions) affects the energy transition and has implications
for climate scenario design. Page returned 403. Addresses how fragmentation modifies energy
price paths, supply chain costs for renewables, and the effectiveness of coordinated vs
uncoordinated climate policies -- all directly relevant to the project's macroeconomic
stress-test scenarios.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Geoeconomic fragmentation is a key macroeconomic risk to the renewable
investment outlook. This note provides the framework for incorporating geopolitical risk
into the stress-test scenario design.

## Possible Application

- Geopolitical risk scenario for the stress-testing framework
- Supply chain cost adjustments for renewable capex under fragmentation
- Scenario design guidance for "fragmented world" stress-test case
