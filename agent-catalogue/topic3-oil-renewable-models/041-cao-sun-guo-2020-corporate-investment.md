# Cao, Sun, Guo (2020) -- Asymmetric Effect of Oil Price Uncertainty on Corporate Investment

**Authors:** Hong Cao, Pengfei Sun, Litian Guo
**Journal:** Frontiers in Energy Research
**Year:** 2020

## Description

Fixed-effects regression study of listed Chinese renewable energy companies (2000-2017)
examining asymmetric oil price uncertainty effects on corporate investment. Findings:
oil price uncertainty reduces overall corporate investment. Asymmetry by firm quality:
firms with superior investment opportunities show positive investment response when oil prices
and uncertainty rise; firms with weaker prospects reduce investment efficiency. Rising oil
price volatility increases risk of over-investment.

## Key Findings

- Oil price uncertainty reduces renewable corporate investment overall
- Asymmetric firm-level responses depend on investment opportunity quality
- Over-investment risk rises with oil price volatility (especially for strong-opportunity firms)

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Firm-level channel between oil price uncertainty and renewable corporate
investment, directly applicable to the microeconomics model. Asymmetric effects by firm
quality inform scenario heterogeneity in stress-testing.

## Possible Application

- Firm-level investment decision framework for the microeconomics model
- Asymmetric uncertainty effects for scenario stress-testing
- Corporate governance moderating variable in investment model
