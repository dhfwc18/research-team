# Esmaeili et al. (2024) -- From Oil Surges to Renewable Shifts

**Authors:** Parisa Esmaeili, Meysam Rafei, Mahmoud Salari, Daniel Balsalobre-Lorente
**Journal:** Energy Policy
**Year:** September 2024

## Description

Uses SVAR and Time-Varying Parameter VAR models to decompose global oil market disruptions
into supply-side and demand-side components and examine their distinct effects on US renewable
energy consumption (January 1973 - May 2021). Key findings: supply disruptions have negligible
renewable effects except from Saudi production or recession-driven shocks; aggregate demand
shocks reduce renewable investment; oil-specific demand shocks initially reduce but eventually
drive renewable consumption.

## Key Findings

- Supply vs demand shock decomposition yields different renewable investment impacts
- Aggregate demand shocks (geopolitical crises) undermine renewable investment
- Oil-specific demand shocks: initial negative, eventual positive renewable effect

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Directly implements the Kilian (2009) shock decomposition in the context of
renewable energy, creating a bridge between the oil price literature and renewable investment.
The TVP-VAR captures time-varying effects relevant to changing energy landscapes.

## Possible Application

- SVAR framework for shock decomposition in the econometric model
- TVP-VAR for time-varying coefficient estimation
- US empirical benchmark for oil shock-renewable investment relationship
