# Zaghdoudi et al. (2023) -- Asymmetric Connectedness Between Oil Price, Coal and Renewable Energy in China

**Authors:** Taha Zaghdoudi, Kais Tissaoui, Mohamed Hedi Maaloul, Younes Bahou, Niazi Kammoun
**Journal:** Energy
**Year:** December 2023

## Description

Uses a Fourier Nonlinear ARDL (NARDL) model to analyse asymmetric connectedness between
oil prices, coal consumption, and renewable energy in China. Key findings: (1) oil-energy
connections are nonlinear; (2) oil price increases have significant short-run effects on coal
consumption; (3) oil price rises boost renewable energy adoption over longer timeframes;
(4) coal consumption follows inverted U-shaped relationship with growth (EKC-type).

## Key Findings

- Oil price increases boost renewable adoption in China over the long run
- Short-run coal effects dominate; long-run renewable substitution follows
- Nonlinear (Fourier) specification captures structural breaks better than linear models

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Provides empirical evidence on the asymmetric and non-linear oil-renewable
relationship in China. The short-run/long-run asymmetry is directly relevant to the stress-
testing time horizons in the project.

## Possible Application

- Non-linear NARDL framework for the econometric model
- Short-run vs long-run distinction for scenario stress-testing
- China-specific evidence for emerging market dynamics
