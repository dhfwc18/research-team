# Ma and Stern (2016) -- Long-Run Estimates of Interfuel and Interfactor Elasticities

**Authors:** Chunbo Ma; David I. Stern
**Journal:** Resource and Energy Economics
**Year:** 2016

## Description

Study estimating long-run interfuel substitution and interfactor (energy-capital-labour)
elasticities using macro panel data. SSRN returned 403 on fetch. Based on citation record,
the paper uses a translog cost function approach and finds evidence for both interfuel
and interfactor substitution with long-run elasticities larger than short-run.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Long-run elasticities are essential for a model assessing structural effects
of Brent price changes on renewable investment over extended time horizons. Both interfuel
(oil vs renewable) and interfactor (energy vs capital) dimensions are relevant.

## Possible Application

- Long-run interfuel elasticity estimates for model parameterisation
- Capital-energy substitution for investment cost modelling
- Panel data methodology reference for econometric analysis
