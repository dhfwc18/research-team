# Ozdurak (2021) -- Nexus Between Crude Oil Prices, Clean Energy Investments and Technology

**Author:** Caner Ozdurak
**Journal:** Green Finance
**Year:** 2021

## Description

DCC-GARCH analysis of volatility spillovers between S&P Global Clean Energy Index, Brent
crude oil futures, CBOE Volatility Index (VIX), and NASDAQ 100 Technology Sector (2009-2021).
Key findings: oil price declines increase market fear (VIX); renewable investment follows
oil prices directionally (both fall when oil falls); technology sector and clean energy returns
are positively correlated.

## Key Findings

- Renewable investment follows oil price directionally in the short run
- DCC-GARCH captures time-varying correlations between oil and clean energy investment
- VIX (market fear) intermediates the oil-clean energy relationship

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- DCC-GARCH framework is directly applicable to the volatility modelling
component. The finding that clean energy investment co-moves with oil prices (rather than
substituting) in the short run is an important empirical constraint for the model.

## Possible Application

- DCC-GARCH framework for volatility spillover modelling in the Backend Engineer's library
- Short-run co-movement vs long-run substitution distinction for scenario design
- VIX as financial channel moderating variable
