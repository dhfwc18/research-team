# Mukhtarov et al. (2024) -- Oil Prices and the Renewable Energy Transition: Evidence from China

**Authors:** Shahriyar Mukhtarov (et al.; Korea University, Khazar University, Baku Engineering University)
**Journal:** Utilities Policy
**Year:** December 2024

## Description

Empirical study of the relationship between oil prices and renewable energy consumption in
China using canonical cointegrating regression (CCR), spanning 1990-2020 (30 years).
Key finding: a 1% increase in oil prices yields a 0.16% increase in renewable energy
consumption. GDP growth (+0.39%) and CO2 emissions (+1.70%) also positively associated.
Rising oil prices make renewables comparatively more attractive due to cost substitution.

## Key Metric

- Oil price elasticity of renewable consumption in China: +0.16%

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Provides a specific, empirically-estimated elasticity of renewable energy
consumption with respect to oil prices. This is a direct input for the project's
microeconomics model calibration.

## Possible Application

- Oil price elasticity of renewable investment/consumption for model calibration
- CCR cointegration methodology reference for long-run relationship estimation
- China case for emerging market comparison
