# Magazzino and Giolli (2024) -- Oil Prices and Renewable Energy Sources in Italy

**Authors:** Cosimo Magazzino (Roma Tre University); Lorenzo Giolli (European School of Economics)
**Journal:** Renewable Energy Focus
**Year:** March 2024

## Description

Multi-method time series study of the oil price-renewable energy relationship in Italy during
the COVID-19 pandemic first wave (January-September 2020). Methods: long memory testing,
spectral causality analysis, wavelet analysis. Key findings: oil prices and renewables were
highly correlated during the pandemic shock; renewable sources (solar, hydro, wind) influenced
oil prices rather than vice versa during this period, demonstrating energy transition's market
impact.

## Key Findings

- High correlation between oil prices and Italian renewable energy during COVID shock
- Direction: renewables influenced oil prices (not the reverse) in this period
- Multi-method (wavelet, spectral causality) approach captures time-frequency dynamics

## Usefulness Assessment

**Partially relevant** -- Country-specific (Italy) and shock-period (COVID) study limits
generalisability. However, wavelet and spectral causality methods are useful for the
time-varying analysis component. The reverse causality finding is noteworthy.

## Possible Application

- Wavelet analysis methodology for time-frequency decomposition in the econometric model
- Reverse causality testing framework
- COVID-period energy market dynamics as a case study episode
