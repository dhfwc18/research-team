# Aziz and Jahan (2023) -- Determinants of International Development Investments in Renewable Energy in Developing Countries

**Authors:** Shakila Aziz (United International University, Bangladesh); Sheikh Morshed Jahan (University of Dhaka, Bangladesh)
**Journal:** Energy for Sustainable Development
**Year:** June 2023

## Description

Panel data study of 86 developing countries (2010-2017) examining determinants of
international development finance flows to renewable energy. Key findings: regulatory
frameworks (FITs, tendering, public investment) positively drive investment; energy poverty
alone does not attract funds; energy dependence positively correlated with inflows; policy
instruments affect loans and grants differently. Heterogeneous effects across development
stages identified.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Provides empirical evidence on the non-price determinants of renewable
investment in developing countries. Useful for separating oil price effects from institutional
and policy factors in the econometric model.

## Possible Application

- Control variable selection for panel regression (policy, institutional quality)
- Framework for understanding heterogeneous oil-price effects across country types
- Reference for regulatory determinants of renewable investment
