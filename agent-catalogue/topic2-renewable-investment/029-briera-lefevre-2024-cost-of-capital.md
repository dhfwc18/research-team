# Briera and Lefevre (2024) -- Reducing the Cost of Capital Through International Climate Finance

**Authors:** Thibault Briera; Julien Lefevre
**Journal:** Energy Policy
**Year:** 2024 (May, Volume 188)

## Description

Paper using the IMACLIM-R multi-regional IAM to examine how international climate finance
(via development finance institutions) reduces financing costs for Variable Renewable Energy
(VRE). Key finding: deep risk-sharing mechanisms could increase Africa's VRE electricity
generation by +43% by 2030 vs baseline. Interaction between public and private capital
modelled through a dedicated cost of capital (CoC) module.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- The cost of capital is a central parameter in renewable investment decisions.
This paper provides the mechanism linking international finance, cost of capital, and renewable
deployment -- directly applicable to the investment model's financial channel.

## Possible Application

- Cost of capital parameterisation for the renewable investment model
- International climate finance as moderating variable on oil price effects
- Africa-specific context for developing country analysis
