# REN21 -- Renewables 2024 Global Status Report: Investment and Finance Module

**Organisation:** REN21 (Renewable Energy Policy Network for the 21st Century)
**Date:** 2024 (data covering 2023)

## Description

Investment and finance module of the REN21 GSR 2024, a multi-stakeholder annual assessment
of the global renewable energy landscape. Key data: global renewable power and fuels
investment reached USD 622.5 billion in 2023, up 8.1% from 2022. Meeting Paris Agreement
targets requires USD 1,300-1,350 billion annually by 2030 (2.36x current levels). Data
sources aggregated: IEA, IRENA, BNEF, World Bank, OECD-DAC. Green bond issuance: USD 575
billion in 2023. Development finance for renewable generation: USD 7.8 billion in 2022.

## Key Metrics

- Renewable investment 2023: USD 622.5 billion (+8.1% YoY)
- Required by 2030: USD 1,300-1,350 billion/year (Paris-aligned)
- Mitigation finance going to renewables (2021/2022): 43%

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Aggregated multi-source investment figure with detailed methodology notes.
Useful for cross-validation of IRENA and IEA investment series. Investment gap analysis
(current vs Paris-aligned) is directly relevant to scenario stress-testing.

## Possible Application

- Investment gap analysis for scenario stress-testing
- Cross-validation of other investment data sources
- 2023 investment benchmark and data source triangulation
