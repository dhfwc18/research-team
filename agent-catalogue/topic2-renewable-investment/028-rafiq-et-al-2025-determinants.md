# Rafiq et al. (2025) -- Navigating the Energy Crisis: Unveiling the Determinants of Renewable Energy Investment

**Authors:** Muhammad Umair Rafiq, Shaham Saleem, Hafsa Bashir, Faiqa Shafqat
**Journal:** Energy
**Year:** 2025

## Description

Panel study of 23 developing and developed nations (2000-2020) using Method of Moments
Quantile Regression. Examines four determinants of renewable investment: AI (consistently
enhances RE investment), energy uncertainty (elevates investment), energy depletion
(significantly boosts investment, diminishing returns at high levels), and financial
development (restricts smaller investments but essential for large-scale projects). Findings
vary across investment scale and country development level.

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Identifies energy uncertainty as a driver of renewable investment, directly
linking oil price uncertainty to investment decisions. The quantile regression approach reveals
non-linearities relevant to the stress-testing component.

## Possible Application

- Energy uncertainty channel from oil prices to renewable investment
- Non-linear effects at different investment scales for scenario design
- Financial development as moderating variable
