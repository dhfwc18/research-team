# Kilian (2009) -- Not All Oil Price Shocks Are Alike

**Author:** Lutz Kilian
**Institution:** University of Michigan
**Journal:** American Economic Review
**Date:** June 2009

## Description

Seminal paper that decomposes crude oil price changes into three structural shock types:
(1) oil supply disruptions, (2) global industrial commodity demand fluctuations, and
(3) oil-market-specific demand shocks. Demonstrates that each shock type has different
macroeconomic consequences. Finds that demand-driven price rises (e.g. 2008) have different
recession implications than supply shocks. Uses a structural VAR model with sign restrictions.

## Key Findings

- Price changes driven by demand behave fundamentally differently from supply shocks
- Regressions of macroeconomic aggregates on oil prices are unstable due to changing shock composition
- 2008 oil price surge was primarily demand-driven and did not cause major U.S. recession at that point

## Usefulness Assessment

**Relevant** -- Foundational for the microeconomics model. Provides the theoretical and
empirical basis for decomposing Brent price changes into supply vs demand components,
which determines the direction and magnitude of the effect on renewable investment.

## Possible Application

- Structural VAR framework for oil price shock decomposition
- Basis for identifying shock types in the historical Brent series
- Theoretical grounding for why oil price impacts on renewables differ by shock origin
