# Hamilton (2008) -- Understanding Crude Oil Prices

**Author:** James D. Hamilton
**Institution:** NBER / University of California San Diego
**Date:** November 2008

## Description

Foundational academic paper examining the determinants of crude oil prices. The PDF is 442 KB;
binary content could not be text-extracted on fetch. Hamilton reviews global oil supply
constraints, demand growth (especially from emerging markets), and the role of speculation.
The paper is widely cited in energy economics and is essential reading for any oil price
modelling effort. Typically covers demand and supply elasticities, price forecasting methodology,
and price-macroeconomy transmission channels.

**Relevant** -- Foundational theoretical and empirical framework for modelling oil price
